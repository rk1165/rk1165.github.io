### Mean Reversion

- In finance, mean reversion is the assumption that a stock's price will tend to move to the average price over time.
- Using mean reversion in stock price analysis involves both identifying the trading range for a stock and computing the average price using analytical techniques taking into account considerations such as earnings, etc.
- When the current market price is less than the average price, the stock is considered attractive for purchase, with the expectation that the price will rise. When the current market price is above the average price, the market price is expected to fall. In other words, deviations from the average price are expected to revert to the average.

# General Theory of Reflexivity

- The situations that has thinking participants, the particpants view of the world is always partial and distorted. That's the principle of fallibility.
- These distorted views can influence the situations to which they relate, because false views lead to inappropriate actions. That's the principle of reflexivity.
- Reason is the slave of passion.
- Our understanding of the world in which we live, is inherently imperfect.
- The concept of reflexivity applies exclusively to situations which have thinking participants. The participant's thinking serves two functions. One is to understand the world in which we live called (Cognitive function). The other is to change the situation to our advantage called the manipulating or participating function.
- The two functions connect thinking and reality in opposite directions.
- In cognitive function reality is supposed to determine participant's views. the direction of causation is from the world to the mind.
- In the manp. function the direction of causation is from the mind to the world. that is to say the intentions of participants have an effect on the world.
- When both functions operate at same time they can interfere with each other. How? By depriving each function of the independent variable that would be needed to determine the value of the dependent variable.
- Objective and subective aspects of reality.
- Thinking constitutes the subjective aspect of reality. This covers what takes place in the minds of the participants.
- Events the objective. What takes place in external reality.
- Negative feedback loop brings participant's views and the actual situations closer together. Positive feedback loops drives them further apart.
- Neg. feedback loop is self-correcting. It can go on forever if there are no significant change in external reality, it may eventually lead to an equilibrium where the participant's views come to correspond to the actual state of affairs. that is what's suppose to happen in financial markets.
- pos. feedback process is self-reinforcing. It can't go on forever because eventually the pv will become so far removed from objective reality that the pv would have to recognise them as unrealistic. Nor can the iterative process occur without change in the actual state of affairs because it is in the nature of pos. feedback that it re-enforces whatever tendency prevails in the real world. Insteas of equilibrium we are faced with a dynamic disequilibrium or far from equilibrium condiions.
